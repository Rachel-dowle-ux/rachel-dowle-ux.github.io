My UX Ethos

The Drop Curb Principle & WCAG: Designing for Everyone

The Drop Curb Principle (also known as the Curb-Cut Effect) suggests that designing for people with specific needs often benefits everyone. Originally coined in relation to sloped pavement curbs for wheelchair users, the same idea applies to digital design — accessible solutions improve usability for all.

For me, WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) isn’t a checkbox. Following WCAG ensures that digital spaces are Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust. These aren’t just principles, they’re tools for designing products that respect people’s diverse needs and contexts.

Whether I’m building search functionality, refining colour contrast, or simplifying navigation, accessibility is part of my process from the start; not an afterthought.
