CASE STUDY

British Sailing Team website

Modernising a high-performance team website and launching the Paris 2024 campaign

Client

Royal Yachting Association (RYA)

Project

British Sailing Team website redesign and Olympic campaign

British Sailing Team website colour pallete

Paris 2024 colour pallete

Project overview

The British Sailing Team is the UK’s national sailing team, representing the pinnacle of Olympic and elite sailing. In the lead-up to the Paris 2024 Olympics, the RYA needed a more maintainable, contemporary website to showcase athletes, events, and campaigns.

I led the UX and frontend design for the new British Sailing Team site—migrating content from WordPress to a streamlined Umbraco CMS, modernising the design in line with a refreshed RYA brand, and creating a flexible system for promoting the team’s Olympic journey.

Objectives

Before redesigning the site, we defined key goals to guide the project

 
✔
Simplify Content Management
Transition from WordPress to a lightweight Umbraco instance with low-code customisation to reduce complexity and maintenance overhead.
✔
Align with Brand Refresh
Incorporate the latest RYA brand updates while maintaining key British Sailing Team visual elements.
✔
Improve UX and Navigation
Reorganise content to better serve journalists, fans, and internal teams looking for athlete bios, event info, and team history.
✔
Deliver Olympic Campaign
Build a structure to support a Paris 2024 campaign, including event schedules, class overviews, and athlete profiles.

My process

1. Discovery and content audit

The project began with a full audit of the legacy WordPress site. I identified essential pages, outdated content, and duplication. This helped shape a more focused content strategy and informed the new information architecture.

I also met with internal comms and performance teams to understand the needs of both public users and internal editors—especially around updating news, athlete profiles, and campaign content quickly.

2. CMS migration to lightweight Umbraco

With the WordPress contract ending, we moved to a pared-down version of Umbraco that enabled simple, low-code content updates. I worked closely with the development team to define flexible templates for news, athlete profiles, and landing pages—reducing reliance on technical support and empowering internal editors.

3. Design refresh guided by brand update

Using the RYA’s recent brand refresh as a foundation, I updated the visual design to feel cleaner, more modern, and better suited to responsive layouts. I balanced the new brand elements (typography, colour palette) with familiar British Sailing Team motifs—like the red/blue colour scheme and photography of athletes in action—to maintain visual continuity.

4. UX-focused component design

I created reusable components in Figma for hero sections, athlete cards, CTA blocks, and content grids. These formed the basis of a simple design system that supported consistent, on-brand layouts and easier development.

Navigation and page hierarchy were restructured around key user goals: finding out about the team, understanding Olympic classes, and accessing news and campaign updates.

5. Olympic campaign buildout

For the Paris 2024 campaign, I designed and built pages highlighting:

  • Each Olympic sailing class, with explainer content and race schedules
  • Athlete profiles with biographies, photos, and achievements
  • Team history, showcasing past Olympic successes
  • News and media updates in the run-up to the Games

These pages used the modular system developed earlier, ensuring rapid build and easy updates by non-technical staff.

Outcomes

The relaunched British Sailing Team website offered a fresh, responsive experience in time for the 2024 Olympics, and provided a scalable foundation for future campaigns and updates.

  • Simplified content workflow: Umbraco CMS enabled internal editors to manage updates independently, with minimal training.
  • Modern, cohesive design: The updated visual language strengthened the connection to the RYA brand while maintaining the British Sailing Team’s identity.
  • Improved UX and structure: Clearer navigation, faster loading pages, and mobile optimisation supported a wider audience.
  • Olympic-ready platform: The new pages supported promotion of the Paris 2024 campaign across news, social media, and broadcast coverage.
  • Positive feedback from internal teams: Editors and stakeholders praised the ease of use, visual polish, and flexibility of the new system.

This project not only ensured a smooth platform migration, but also elevated the team’s digital presence in one of their most important years.
